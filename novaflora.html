➔ NOVA FLORA
➔ Presentation of a series of science fiction posters to highlight La Digitale from Nova Flora, the flora of 6500 years after J.-C.
➔ The series consists of three posters: a schematic poster, an explanatory poster, and a decorative poster. I worked on a single flower to be able to develop the entire theoretical part without having to generalize to other similar plants. The goal is to have a scientific tone that is sufficiently explanatory and didactic so that the concept is understood by the youngest, while being credible. The colors chosen contrast with the scientific tone thanks to a less cold approach to the subject. They are also linked to the modification of the nature of the digitalis. These three primary colors are not very present in nature, which helps to dissipate the ambiguity of the reality of the flower. We therefore understand that it is a hypothesis of flora. The poster is largely empty, which translates a poor environment where it is difficult for flowers to thrive. The digitalis seems alone in the world.
➔ x3 Din-A3 Risographed posters.
